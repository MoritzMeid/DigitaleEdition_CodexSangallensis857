The Old English story of Apollonius of Tyre , is considered the first English novel. It is stored in the manuscript CCCC 201 at the Parker Library in Cambridge. The manuscript contains a huge number of texts, mainly homilies by Wulfstan. The texts are written in both Latin and Old English. The date of the manuscript is the half of the 11th century.

The story of Apollonius starts with an incest episode and proceeds telling the adventure of Apollonius, the young and cultivated prince of Tyre. At the end of his adventures, which take many years, he will become a rex iustus.
